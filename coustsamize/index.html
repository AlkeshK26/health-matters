Body Mass Index (BMI) is a calculation that expresses the relationship between your height and weight as a single number. It's an estimate of body fat and a good measure of your risk for diseases that can occur with overweight and obesity. BMI categories are: BMI Categories Underweight: BMI < 18.5 Normal weight: BMI = 18.5-24.9 Overweight: BMI = 25-29.9 Obese: BMI ≥ 30 The BMI is calculated by dividing your weight in kilograms by your height in meters squared (kg/m2). BMI is an indicator of total body fat in many individuals. It is more of an indicator than a direct measurement of a person’s total body fat. This means that as the BMI score increases, so does a person’s total body fat. The WHO defines an adult who has a BMI between 25 and 29.9 as overweight - an adult who has a BMI of 30 or higher is considered obese - a BMI below 18.5 is considered underweight, and between 18.5 to 24.9 a healthy weight.
