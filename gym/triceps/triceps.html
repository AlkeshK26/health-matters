SKULL EXTENSION

Steps and Benefits of Skull Extension for Triceps

Steps:

  • Step 1: Lie on a flat bench, holding a dumbbell or barbell over your chest with your arms extended and your hands shoulder-width apart.
  • Step 2: Lower the weight down towards your forehead, keeping your upper arms still and only moving your forearms, until your arms are bent at a 90-degree angle.
  • Step 3: Hold the weight at the bottom of the movement for a brief moment, focusing on stretching your triceps.
  • Step 4: Extend your arms back to the starting position, squeezing your triceps at the top of the movement.
  • Step 5: Repeat for the desired number of reps and sets, increasing the weight or reps as you build strength and endurance.

Benefits:

  • Targets the triceps brachii muscles, specifically the lateral and medial heads.
  • Works the anconeus muscle, assisting in elbow extension.
  • Improves overall triceps strength, size, and development.
  • Enhances athletic performance and reduces the risk of elbow injuries.
  • Can help improve lockout strength and overall upper body strength.
  • Is a versatile exercise that can be performed with a variety of equipment, including dumbbells, barbells, and cables.

CABLE PUSH DOWN

Steps and Benefits of Cable Pushdown for Triceps

Steps:

  • Step 1: Stand facing a cable machine with the cable at chest height, holding a bar with your hands shoulder-width apart and your arms extended.
  • Step 2: Press the bar down towards your thighs, keeping your elbows close to your body and only moving your forearms, until your arms are fully extended.
  • Step 3: Hold the bar at the bottom of the movement for a brief moment, focusing on contracting your triceps.
  • Step 4: Return the bar to the starting position, keeping control throughout the movement, and repeat for the desired number of reps and sets.
  • Step 5: Increase the weight or reps as you build strength and endurance.

Benefits:

  • Targets the triceps brachii muscles, specifically the lateral and medial heads.
  • Works the anconeus muscle, assisting in elbow extension.
  • Improves overall triceps strength, size, and development.
  • Enhances athletic performance and reduces the risk of elbow injuries.
  • Can help improve lockout strength and overall upper body strength.
  • Is a versatile exercise that can be performed with a variety of equipment, including cables and ropes.
  • Allows for a range of grip widths and stances, making it suitable for different training goals and preferences.

OVERHEAD EXTENSION

Steps and Benefits of Overhead Extension for Triceps

Steps:

  • Step 1: Stand or sit with your feet shoulder-width apart, holding a dumbbell or barbell overhead with your arms extended and your hands shoulder-width apart.
  • Step 2: Lower the weight behind your head, keeping your upper arms still and only moving your forearms, until your arms are bent at a 90-degree angle.
  • Step 3: Hold the weight at the bottom of the movement for a brief moment, focusing on stretching your triceps.
  • Step 4: Extend your arms back to the starting position, squeezing your triceps at the top of the movement.
  • Step 5: Repeat for the desired number of reps and sets, increasing the weight or reps as you build strength and endurance.

Benefits:

  • Targets the triceps brachii muscles, specifically the long head.
  • Works the anconeus muscle, assisting in elbow extension.
  • Improves overall triceps strength, size, and development.
  • Enhances athletic performance and reduces the risk of elbow injuries.
  • Can help improve lockout strength and overall upper body strength.
  • Is a versatile exercise that can be performed with a variety of equipment, including dumbbells, barbells, and cables.
  • Allows for a range of grip widths and stances, making it suitable for different training goals and preferences.
  • Can be performed seated or standing, making it accessible for those with mobility limitations.
