LATERAL RAISE

Steps and Benefits of Lateral Raises

Steps:

  • Step 1: Stand or sit with your feet shoulder-width apart, holding dumbbells at your sides with your palms facing your thighs.
  • Step 2: Keeping your arms straight, lift the dumbbells out to the sides until they are at shoulder height, with your palms facing the floor.
  • Step 3: Hold the dumbbells at shoulder height for a brief moment, squeezing your shoulder muscles.
  • Step 4: Lower the dumbbells back to the starting position, keeping control throughout the movement.
  • Step 5: Repeat for the desired number of reps and sets.

Benefits:

  • Targets the deltoid muscles, specifically the medial (side) deltoids.
  • Works the trapezius and rotator cuff muscles.
  • Improves overall shoulder strength, stability, and development.
  • Enhances athletic performance and reduces the risk of shoulder injuries.
  • Can help improve posture by strengthening the muscles that support the shoulders and upper back.

INCLINE T RAISE

Steps and Benefits of Incline T Raise

Steps:

  • Step 1: Sit on an incline bench with your feet flat on the floor, holding dumbbells with your arms extended and your palms facing each other, forming a "T" shape.
  • Step 2: Keeping your arms straight, lift the dumbbells out to the sides, maintaining the "T" shape, and squeeze your upper back muscles.
  • Step 3: Hold the dumbbells at the top of the movement for a brief moment, focusing on contracting your upper back muscles.
  • Step 4: Lower the dumbbells back to the starting position, keeping control throughout the movement.
  • Step 5: Repeat for the desired number of reps and sets.

Benefits:

  • Targets the upper back muscles, specifically the trapezius, rhomboids, and rear deltoids.
  • Works the rotator cuff muscles and improves shoulder stability.
  • Improves overall upper body strength and development.
  • Enhances athletic performance and reduces the risk of shoulder and upper back injuries.
  • Can help improve posture by strengthening the muscles that support the shoulders and upper back.

T BAR ROW

Steps and Benefits of Exercise

  • Step 1: Adjust the incline of the bench to a 45-degree angle
  • Step 2: Grip the bar with your hands slightly wider than shoulder-width apart
  • Step 3: Lower the bar to your upper chest, then press upwards until your arms are fully extended
  • Step 4: Repeat for the desired number of reps and sets

Benefits:

  • Targets the upper chest muscles (pectoralis major)
  • Works shoulders and triceps
  • Improves overall upper body strength and development

LAT PULL DOWN

Steps and Benefits of Exercise

  • Step 1: Stand facing a cable machine with the cable at chest height
  • Step 2: Hold a handle in each hand with your arms extended to the sides
  • Step 3: Press the handles out to the sides, keeping your arms straight
  • Step 4: Repeat for the desired number of reps and sets

Benefits:

  • Targets the chest muscles (pectoralis major) from a different angle
  • Works shoulders and improves overall upper body flexibility
  • Improves chest development and symmetry

CABLE ROW

Steps and Benefits of Exercise

  • Step 1: Sit on a hammer strength machine with your back against the pad
  • Step 2: Grip the handles with your palms facing each other
  • Step 3: Press the handles together, extending your arms fully
  • Step 4: Repeat for the desired number of reps and sets

Benefits:

  • Targets the chest muscles (pectoralis major) from a different angle
  • Works shoulders and triceps
  • Improves overall upper body strength and development

REVERSE FLY

Steps and Benefits of Exercise

  • Step 1: Adjust the Machine
  • Step 2: Grip the handles with your palms facing each other
  • Step 3: Lower the Handles
  • Step 4: Push Back Up & repeat

Benefits:

  • The exercise targets the chest muscles from a different angle, which is a great variation from traditional dips.
  • It also engages the shoulders and triceps
  • Improves overall upper body strength and development
