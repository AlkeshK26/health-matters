STANDING CRUNCH

Steps and Benefits of Standing Crunch

Steps:

  • Step 1: Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, toes facing forward or slightly outward
  • Step 2: Interlock your fingers and place your hands behind your head for support, but avoid pulling on your head or neck
  • Step 3: Engage your core muscles by drawing your belly button towards your spine
  • Step 4: Slowly lift your shoulders off the ground, curling up towards your knees, keeping your lower back straight
  • Step 5: Hold the crunch for a brief moment (about 1-2 seconds) at the top of the movement
  • Step 6: Slowly lower your shoulders back down to the starting position, maintaining control throughout the movement
  • Step 7: Repeat for the desired number of reps and sets

Benefits:

  • Targets the rectus abdominis muscle, helping to improve core strength and stability
  • Works the obliques, which can help with rotational movement and overall athletic performance
  • Improves posture by strengthening the muscles that support the spine
  • Can help reduce the risk of lower back injuries by strengthening the core muscles
  • Improves overall athletic performance by increasing power, speed, and endurance

CABLE TWIST

Steps and Benefits of Cable Twist

Steps:

  • Step 1: Stand facing a cable machine with the cable at chest height, holding a handle in both hands
  • Step 2: Hold the handle with your arms extended and your hands shoulder-width apart, with your feet shoulder-width apart and toes facing forward or slightly outward
  • Step 3: Twist your torso to one side, keeping your arms straight, and focus on squeezing your obliques
  • Step 4: Slowly return to the starting position, maintaining control throughout the movement
  • Step 5: Repeat on the other side by twisting your torso in the opposite direction
  • Step 6: Continue alternating sides for the desired number of reps and sets

Benefits:

  • Targets the obliques, helping to improve core strength and rotational movement
  • Works the entire core, including the rectus abdominis and transverse abdominis muscles
  • Improves posture by strengthening the muscles that support the spine
  • Can help reduce the risk of lower back injuries by strengthening the core muscles
  • Improves overall athletic performance by increasing power, speed, and endurance

SIDE BAND

Steps and Benefits of Side Band

Steps:

  • Step 1: Hold a resistance band in both hands at shoulder height, with the band at a 90-degree angle to your body
  • Step 2: Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, toes facing forward or slightly outward
  • Step 3: Press the band away from your body, keeping your arms straight, and focus on squeezing your shoulder blades together
  • Step 4: Slowly return the band to the starting position, maintaining control throughout the movement
  • Step 5: Repeat on the other side by switching the position of the band and repeating steps 3-4
  • Step 6: Continue alternating sides for the desired number of reps and sets

Benefits:

  • Targets the deltoid muscles, helping to improve shoulder strength and stability
  • Works the rotator cuff muscles, which can help with injury prevention and overall shoulder health
  • Improves posture by strengthening the muscles that support the scapula
  • Can help reduce the risk of shoulder injuries by strengthening the surrounding muscles
  • Improves overall athletic performance by increasing power, speed, and endurance

CHAIR LEG RAISE

Steps and Benefits of Chair Leg Raise

Steps:

  • Step 1: Sit on the edge of a sturdy chair with your feet flat on the floor, holding onto the armrests for support if needed
  • Step 2: Lift one leg off the ground, keeping it straight, and hold for a brief moment
  • Step 3: Slowly lower your leg back down to the starting position, maintaining control throughout the movement
  • Step 4: Repeat with the other leg, lifting it off the ground and holding for a brief moment
  • Step 5: Continue alternating legs for the desired number of reps and sets
  • Step 6: As you get stronger, you can increase the difficulty by lifting both legs at the same time or adding weights to your legs

Benefits:

  • Targets the abdominal muscles, particularly the lower abs, helping to improve core strength and stability
  • Works the hip flexors and quadriceps, helping to improve flexibility and range of motion
  • Improves balance and coordination by engaging the core and leg muscles
  • Can help reduce the risk of lower back injuries by strengthening the core muscles
  • Improves overall athletic performance by increasing power, speed, and endurance

WEIGHTED RUSSIAN TWIST & CRUNCH

Steps and Benefits of Exercise

Steps and Benefits of Weighted Russian Twist & Crunch

Steps:

  • Step 1: Sit on the floor with your knees bent and feet flat, holding a weight or medicine ball in front of your chest
  • Step 2: Lean back slightly and lift your feet off the ground, keeping them a few inches apart
  • Step 3: Twist your torso to one side, touching the weight to the ground beside you, and crunch your abdominal muscles
  • Step 4: Hold for a brief moment, then twist to the other side, touching the weight to the ground on the other side of your body
  • Step 5: Continue alternating sides for the desired number of reps and sets
  • Step 6: Focus on engaging your core muscles and maintaining control throughout the movement
  • Step 7: As you get stronger, you can increase the difficulty by adding more weight or increasing the number of reps and sets

Benefits:

  • Targets the obliques and entire core, helping to improve rotational movement and overall core strength
  • Works the rectus abdominis and transverse abdominis muscles, helping to improve posture and reduce the risk of lower back injuries
  • Improves athletic performance by increasing power, speed, and endurance
  • Enhances functional movement and coordination by engaging the core and leg muscles
  • Can help burn belly fat and reveal a more defined midsection
  • Improves overall muscle balance and stability
