    SHOULDERS EXERCISE

Chest Exercises

SHOULDER PRESS

Steps and Benefits of Exercise

  • Step 1: Adjust the incline of the bench to a 45-degree angle
  • Step 2: Grip the bar with your hands slightly wider than shoulder-width apart
  • Step 3: Lower the bar to your upper chest, then press upwards until your arms are fully extended
  • Step 4: Repeat for the desired number of reps and sets

Benefits:

  • Targets the upper chest muscles (pectoralis major)
  • Works shoulders and triceps
  • Improves overall upper body strength and development

CLOSE GRIP SHOULDER PRESS

Steps and Benefits of Exercise

  • Step 1: Sit on a pec deck machine with your back against the pad
  • Step 2: Hold the handles with your arms bent and your elbows at a 90-degree angle
  • Step 3: Press the handles together, squeezing your chest muscles
  • Step 4: Repeat for the desired number of reps and sets

Benefits:

  • Targets the chest muscles (pectoralis major) from a different angle
  • Works shoulders and improves overall upper body flexibility
  • Improves chest development and symmetry

LATERAL RAISE

Steps and Benefits of Exercise

  • Step 1: Lie on a flat bench with your feet planted firmly on the ground

  • Step 4: Repeat for the desired number of reps and sets

Benefits:

  • Builds chest muscle (pectoralis major)
  • Works shoulders and triceps
  • Improves overall upper body strength

REAR FLY

Steps and Benefits of Exercise

  • Step 1: Stand facing a cable machine with the cable at chest height
  • Step 2: Hold a handle in each hand with your arms extended to the sides
  • Step 3: Press the handles out to the sides, keeping your arms straight
  • Step 4: Repeat for the desired number of reps and sets

Benefits:

  • Targets the chest muscles (pectoralis major) from a different angle
  • Works shoulders and improves overall upper body flexibility
  • Improves chest development and symmetry

SCOTT PRESS

Steps and Benefits of Exercise
