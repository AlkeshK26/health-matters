CHAIR LEG RAISE

Steps and Benefits of Chair Leg Raise

Steps:

  • Step 1: Sit on the edge of a sturdy chair with your feet flat on the floor, holding onto the armrests for support if needed.
  • Step 2: Slowly lift one leg off the floor, keeping it straight, and hold for a brief moment.
  • Step 3: Lower your leg back to the starting position, keeping control throughout the movement.
  • Step 4: Repeat with the other leg, alternating legs for the desired number of reps and sets.
  • Step 5: As you get stronger, you can increase the difficulty by lifting both legs at the same time or adding weights to your legs.

Benefits:

  • Targets the abdominal muscles, specifically the lower abs.
  • Works the hip flexor muscles, which assist in lifting the legs.
  • Improves overall core strength and stability.
  • Enhances athletic performance and reduces the risk of lower back injuries.
  • Can help improve balance and coordination.
  • Is a low-impact exercise, making it suitable for people with joint issues or mobility limitations.

CABLE TWIST OVERHEAD PRESS

Steps and Benefits of Exercise

Steps and Benefits of Cable Twist Overhead Press

Steps:

  • Step 1: Stand facing a cable machine with the cable at shoulder height, holding a handle in each hand with your arms extended to the sides at shoulder height.
  • Step 2: Press the handle upwards and outwards, extending your arms fully and twisting your torso to one side, keeping your core muscles engaged.
  • Step 3: Hold the handle at the top of the movement for a brief moment, focusing on contracting your shoulders and triceps.
  • Step 4: Lower the handle back to the starting position, keeping control throughout the movement, and repeat on the other side.
  • Step 5: Alternate sides for the desired number of reps and sets, increasing the weight or reps as you build strength and endurance.

Benefits:

  • Targets the deltoid muscles, specifically the medial and posterior heads.
  • Works the triceps brachii muscles, assisting in elbow extension.
  • Improves overall shoulder strength, stability, and mobility.
  • Enhances athletic performance and reduces the risk of shoulder injuries.
  • Can help improve posture and reduce muscle imbalances in the shoulders and upper back.
  • Engages the core muscles, improving overall core strength and stability.
